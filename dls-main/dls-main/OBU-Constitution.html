OLD BOY'S UNION

 DE  LA  SALLE  COLLEGE   OLD  BOYS’  UNION

( Inaugurated  03rd Feb 1929 )



  1. PROFILE   BASE/ORIENTATION


  1. Name  –  This Union shall be called the “OLD LASALLIANS’ UNION” and shall be known as “DE LA SALLE COLLEGE OLD BOYS’ UNION” for convenience abbreviated DLS OBU (hereinafter referred to as “the Union”.

  2. Address  –  The Address of the Union shall be the Official address of the College (De La College, Mutwal, Colombo-15).

  3. Motto  –  The Motto of the Union shall be the College motto as beneath the Crest “INDIVISA MANENT” meant in English “We Stand Undivided” signifies inseparable brotherhood, a motto of the La Salle family which the Brothers came to cherish.

  4. Crest  –  The Crest of the Union shall be the device depicted in Schedule One. (1)

  5. Colours  –  The Colours of the Union shall be the College colours. It is inclusion in the Coat of Arms, the Virile Red and Gentle Blue is the colours of St. John Baptist De La Salle after whom the school is named. Red too, stands for Valour and Blue for Endurance or Perseverance.

  6. Flag  –   The Flag of the Union shall be as depicted in Schedule two. (2)

  7. Anthem  –  The Anthem of the Union shall be the De La Salle College, Mutwal anthem, as depicted in Schedule three. (3)

  8. Hallmark  –  The hallmark of the Union shall be to maintain “STRICT DISCIPLINE with GREAT KINDNESS”. It is the art of harnessing.




  1. OBJECTS


The Objects of the Union shall be


  1. To maintain the interest of Lasallians and promote loyalty to the Alma Mater.

  2. To foster and preserve the bonds of unity, mutual help and good fellowship among the Old Boys.

  3. To increase the sense of fellowship between the present and old collegians, both with one and another and with the College.

  4. To afford guidance, encouragement and help the present boys and younger generation of De La Salle College in their activities.

  5. To promote and advance Education, Sports, Social, Youth, Culture and Economic welfare, of Lasallians. 

  6. To promote the advancement, progress, economic and general welfare of the Union.

  7. To spread the “Lasallian Spirit” in a positive way to benefit the Students, the Staff, the Administration and as well as all the Old Boys, thereby to support the whole family of De La Salle College.

  8. To liase with all such bodies that are established in respect of, in relation to or in connection with the management, administration, supervision, or development of the College or otherwise relating to the College and assist such bodies in the exercise of respective powers, duties and functions, including the determination of matters of policy for the College and in the implementation of such policy.






  1. MEMBERSHIP


  1. Categories –  The categories are as follows;

  1. Old  Lasallian

  2. Active  Lasallian

  3. Loyal  Lasallian

  4. Grand  Lasallian

  5. Great  Lasallian

  6. Supreme  Lasallian


  1. Eligibility –  The eligibility for a member as follows;

  1. Old  Lasallian  –  Old boys of the College, who have been students for a period of not less than twenty-four (24) months. Provided however, the Council may in it’s absolute discretion and without reason stated, enroll as a member of the Union, an Old Boy of the College, who had been a student thereof for a period less than the said stipulated period.

  2. Active  Lasallian  –  The members of the Union, who shall participate for activities of the Union for a period of three (3) years consecutively, become an Active Lasallian, and who is duly elected as such on a Special Resolution passed at a Council Meeting by a two-third (2/3) majority of Members. 

  3. Loyal  Lasallian  –  Loyal Lasallian may be conferred on a person who has distinguished himself by outstanding services to the Union, and who is duly elected as such on a Special Resolution passed at a Council Meeting by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of Members. 

  4. Grand  Lasallian  –  The Old Boys of the College who have been Teachers,  or members of the tutorial staff of De La Salle College, Mutwal, who shall be immediately on completion of the first ten (10) years become a Grand  Lasallian.

  5. Great  Lasallian – The Great Lasallian is an honour given to the Old Boys of the College who have been socially recognized by the society.    

  6. Supreme  Lasallian  – The Union’s highest honour, Supreme Lasallian is given to a person recognizes commitment to humanitarian work or the Old Boys of the College who have been Priests or Religious.

 

  1. Application   –   An application for membership shall be made on the prescribed form duly proposed and seconded by two existing members, one of whom shall be a member of the Council of the Union. It shall be forwarded to the Chief Organizer of the Union who shall submit it to the Council for consideration.

  2. Enrolment  –   Every application shall be tabled by the Chief Organizer of the Union at a meeting of the Council which it deems necessary or relevant for a proper consideration of the application. The Council of the Union shall have the power to accept or reject such Application. 

  3. Registration  –  The Chief Organizer of the Union shall maintain   a Register of Members according to the respective categories. Any person who had participated for the Annual General Meeting at the time of the approval of this Constitution, shall continue to remain as an Old Lasallian category.

  4. Membership Identity Card  –  The Chief Organizer of the Union shall charge a sum of rupees two hundred to issue the Membership Identity Card, which shall be terminated after a period of two years from the date of issue and can be renewed on the same terms for a further period of two years. 

  5. Resignation  –  Any member who intends to resign from the Union shall notify such intention to the General Secretary of the Union in writing. The resignation shall become affective on acceptance by the Council on such Terms and Conditions as it may determine. Similarly, the Council of the Union shall have the power to readmit in to membership any applicant who seeks readmission.

  6. Discipline  –  The members of the Union shall honour the rules and regulations of the Union and conduct good discipline to maintain the College name. 

  7. Warning, Suspension & Expulsion  –  Any member may be warned, suspended or expelled from membership of the Union by an order of the Council if he is found guilty of any conduct prejudicial to the interests and/or the good name of the Union. Provided that, before an order of warning, suspension or expulsion is made,  the member shall be given an opportunity of showing cause in writing why such an order should not be made.

  8. Removal   –   The Council shall have the power to remove the name of any member from the Register after due notice in case of conduct unworthy of the Union activities even as a Guest of a Member, and his name shall be posted up on the Union Notice Board or the Union news bulletin.      



  1. PATRONAL ADVISORS


  1. Patron  –  The Patron of the Union shall be the Very Rev. Bro. Provincial Visitor of the Brothers of the Christian Schools (FSC) in Sri Lanka. (Ex-Officio)

  2. Vice Patrons  –  The  Vice Patrons  of  the Union  shall be all  the  Rev. Bro. Directors  Emeritus  of De La Salle College. (Ex-Officio)

  3. Spiritual Advisors  –  The Spiritual Advisors shall be the Parish Priests of  St. James’ (Mutwal), St. John’s (Modera), St. Mary’s (Mattakkuliya) and St. Andrew’s (Lunupokuna) Churches. 

  4. Moderator  –  The Moderator shall be a Rev. Brother (FSC) who nominated by the Patron of the Union or an Old Boy who registered as a Supreme Lasallian shall be nominated by the President, in consultation with the Council.

  5. Board of Directors  –  The Directors shall be five (05) distinguished well recognized dedicated Old Boys who registered as Grand Lasallians shall be nominated by the President of the Union, in consultation with the Council.

  6. The above-mentioned Clause 4.1, 4.2, 4.3, 4.4 and 4.5 shall be entitled to attend and actively participate in any Meetings connected with the Union. 



  1. OFFICE  BEARERS


  1. President – Rev.Bro.Director of De La Salle College. (Ex-Officio)

  2. Deputy President – Shall be elected at the AGM of the Union.

  3. Six Vice Presidents – Shall be elected at the AGM and, one of whom shall be the Rev.Bro. Sub-Director of De La Salle College.  (Ex-Offico) 

(A) Vice President - Culture   (Sub Director of DLS)

(B) Vice President - Economy 

(C) Vice President - Education

(D) Vice President - Sports

(E) Vice President - Youth

(F) Vice President - Social


  1. General Secretary – Shall be elected at the AGM

  2. Treasurer – ditto

  3. Chief Organizer –      ditto

  4. Assistant Secretary – ditto

  5. Assistant Treasurer – ditto

  6. All Office Bearers except the President (Ex-Officio) and Vice President (Ex-Officio) shall be elected at the AGM from among members of the Union who have served as members of the Council or any of the Subsidiary Organization for a period of two years and shall hold the office until the next AGM.    

  7. Any vacancy occurring before the next Annual General Meeting, the members of the Council shall elect a successor from among  members of the Union.  

 

  1. EXECUTIVE  MEMBERS


The Executive Members of the Sub-Committees shall be consisting of the following members.

  1. Sports Secretary – Shall be the Secretary of the Sports Committee. 

  2. Social Secretary – Shall be the Secretary of the Social Committee. 

  3. Youth Secretary – Shall be the Secretary of the Youth Committee.

  4. Educational Secretary – Shall be the Secretary of the Educational Services Committee. 

  5. Media Secretary – Shall be the Secretary of the Media Committee.

  6. The above mentioned Clause 6.1, 6.2, 6.3, 6.4, and 6.5 appointed members shall be entitled to appoint Coordinators to be responsible for the conduct of each section which may be expanded from time to time as necessity demands.




  1. ELECTED MEMBERS


Twenty members shall be elected from among the members of the Union at the Annual General Meeting as follows.

  1. Five (5) members shall be under the age of 30 years

  2. Five (5) members shall be between 30 and 40 years of age

  3. Five (5) members shall be between 40 and 50 years of age

  4. Five (5) members shall be over 50 years of age




  1. COUNCIL


The business of the Union shall be managed by a council comprising

  1. The Office Bearers of the Union

  2. The Executive Members of the Union

  3. The Elected Members of the Union

  4. The Council from among members of the Union shall fill any vacancy occurring before the next AGM.

  5. Except De La Salle Brothers, any Clubs, Societies, Communities, Services, Associations, Organizations or other bodies in the name of “Lasallian” deemed to be establish or hereafter establish by the Council.

  6. A member of the Council shall be cease to hold office if he absents himself from three (3) consecutive Council Meetings without prior notice.

  7. In the event of a vacancy occurring in the Council the remaining members shall elect a successor.   




  1. PROCEDURE AT MEETINGS


  1. Committee Meetings – The Sub-Committees or Subsidiary Organizations members shall meet each section once in a month before the Council Meeting or from time to time as necessity arises. The respective Vice Presidents, or in their absence the respective Secretaries shall preside at the Meeting.

  2. Council Meeting – The Council shall meet at least once in every calendar month and at least five (5) days notice shall be given by the General Secretary or the Assistant Secretary of the Union. The President, or in his absence the Deputy President or in his absence one of the Vice Presidents of the Union elected by the members present shall preside at the Council meeting.

  3. Special General Meeting – The Council may any time convene a Special General Meeting on the direction of the President or upon a requisition made by at least twenty five (25) members, and stating the purpose for which the meeting is to be summoned by the General Secretary of the Union, with not less than fourteen (14) days notice.

  4. Annual General Meeting – The Annual General Meeting of the Union shall ordinarily be held on or not later than 30th April of each year. Provided that, the Council may in special circumstances fix the Annual General Meeting for an early date thereafter as is reasonably practical. Not less than three weeks (21 days) notice shall be given to the members by the General Secretary of the Union and the Agenda shall include the following items.  

  1. Tabling of the Audited Statements of Accounts of the Union, the Sports Committee, the Fellowship Committee, the Magazine Committee, the Youth Committee, the Education Committee, any Fund and any Club, Association, Society, Organization and other body established by the Council and of the respective Annual Reports thereof and of the Annual Report of the Finance Committee.

  2. Election of Board of Directors, Office Bearers, Executive Members and Elected Members of the Council for the ensuing year.

  3. Appointment of an Auditor

  4. Resolutions of which not less than seven (7) days notice in writing shall have been given to the General Secretary.

  1. Quorum – The quorum at the Special and Annual General Meeting shall be fifty (50). Provided that should there be no quorum present for an Special or Annual General Meeting, it shall be postponed for a date fourteen (14) days later and shall then be held, irrespectively of the number of members present. Twenty (20) members shall form a quorum at the Council Meeting, provided that, should there be no quorum present for a Council meeting, it shall be postponed for a date five (5) days later and shall then be held irrespective of the number of members present. 

  2. Reports – The Annual Reports, Progress Reports of the sub-committees or sub-organizations together with the Audited Accounts, shall be issued to all Members of the Union at least fourteen (14) days before the AGM.

  3. Agenda – The Agenda for a General Meeting shall be issued to all members of the Union at least seven (7) days before the AGM.

  4. Admittance – Admittance to any General Meeting shall be on presentation of the Membership Card.

  5. Nominations – Any member of the Union may submit a nomination paper proposing the names of candidates for the Council of the Union which shall contain the name, address and membership number of the candidate, proposer and seconder. Such nominations shall be handed over to the General Secretary at or before the AGM.  

  6. Election – If more than the prescribed numbers of nominations have been tendered in respect of any position, an election shall take place.

  7. Voting – No member shall be permitted to vote at the AGM unless he has been a member for a continuous period of at least one month, prior to the date of such meeting. Voting at election shall be by ballot paper or a show of hands, as determined by the election committee.

  8. Preside – The President, or in his absence  any one elected by the members present shall preside at the AGM. And the Election Committee shall be the Patron, Vice Patrons and President.








  1. FINANCE


  1. Financial Year – The Financial Year of the Union shall be from 01st April  to 31st March.

  2. Audit – The Annual Accounts of the Union shall be audited before the 15th January of the year following by the Auditor.

  3. Finance Committee – The Finance Committee shall be consisting of President, Deputy President, General Secretary, Vice President (Economy), Treasurer and Assistant Treasurer of the Union.

  4. Supplementary Accounts – All Special Donations and or any other monies collected by Sub-Committees for various section will be maintained by them in Supplementary Bank Accounts to be opened by them.  Cheques drawn on these Accounts shall be signed by the Treasurer of the Union, together with the respective Vice President or Secretary of the Sub-Committees. At the end of the Financial Year, each Sub-Committee Secretary will hand over to the Finance Committee a Statement of the operation of the Accounts for that year, and make available his books and documents for Audit if necessary. The Treasurer of the Union will incorporate these balances into the AGM.

  5. Expenditure – No expenditure, of non-recurrent or unusual nature shall be incurred without the prior sanction of the Council or any Sub-Committees appointed for a particular purpose.

  6. Custody of Funds –. The Treasurer, together with the President or the Deputy President shall sign cheques or any payments on the Main account. The Treasurer together with the Secretary or the Vice President of respective Sub-Committees shall sign any payments on Supplementary Accounts.

  7. Donations – No donations in cash or any kind shall be solicited without the prior approval of the Council. All donations will be in the custody of the Finance Committee who will be responsible for its operation, together with rendition of the relevant Account. All Donation List shall be issued only under the signature of Treasurer of the Union with the counter signature of the President.




11 ACTIVITIES  OF  THE  COMMITTEES


  1. Sports Committee (Lasallians Sports Club) – The Vice President (Sports) shall be the Chairman of the Sports Committee. The committee shall provide for the benefit of Lasallians, in many activities as are practicable both indoor and outdoors consistent with avowed objects. All activities shall be under the control of the Council, which shall appoint Coordinators to be responsible for the conduct of each Sports Section (Eg: Cricket, Soccer, Rugger, Basketball, Volleyball, Athletics, Swimming, Tennis, Table Tennis, Billiard, Snooker, Chess, etc., etc.,) which may be expanded from time to time as necessity demands.

  2. Fellowship Committee – The Vice President (Social) shall be the Chairman of the Fellowship Committee. The committee shall organize appropriate and interesting Amusements, Social and Entertainment programmes as well as the Annual Get-together, Annual Mass and shall provide honours and respects to the members of the Union.  

  3. Media Committee (Lasallians In Action) – The Activities of the Media Committee shall be to spread the Lasallian Spirit in a positive way to benefit the whole of Lasallian family. It shall publish a bulletin quarter yearly with all the activities and functions of the Union and the College. 

  4. Youth Committee (Lasallian Youth Movement) – The Activities of the Youth Committee is to motivate the youth and encourage leadership.

  5. Educational Committee – The Activities of the Educational Committee shall be to help to promote the advancement of educational talents and cultural progress of the present students and the younger generation of De La Salle College.

  6. Economy Committee – The economy of the Union shall be develop by a committee comprising all the Office Bearers and the Vice President (Economy) whom shall be the Chairman of the Economy Committee.

  7. Finance Committee – The Treasurer of the Union shall be the Chairman of the Finance Committee. The committee shall control the expenditure and prepare budget for each section and shall convene meetings by once in every two months. 

  8. Managing Committee – The Business and affairs of the Union shall be controlled and managed by the Managing Committee consisting of President, Deputy President, Vice Presidents, General Secretary, Treasurer and Chief Organizer.    




12 DUTIES,  RESPONSIBILITIES  &  AUTHORITIES


  1. President – The President is the Administrator of the Union. He shall have the overall power to in any matter connected with the Union, and be entitled and actively participate in all meetings. 

  2. Deputy President – The Deputy President is the Chief Executive Officer (CEO). He shall coordinate the activities of the union, in leadership, project planning, implementation, and evaluation towards the development and keep financial control of them. He should provide directions to the Union to accomplish its objectives. He should be a man of ideas with progressive and vibrant outlook to build a strong team to meet the challenges.  

  3. Vice Presidents – The Vice Presidents are the Officers in Charge for each Object. They should identify the resources both human and financial drawing up programmes of works, thereby setting the goals. Motivate and supervise the Sub-Committee/Sub-Organization to prioritize their activities and shall encourage the executive members and the coordinators respective each Section. Shall direct the vision and commitment of each Sub-Committee/Sub-Organization.

  4. General Secretary – The Success of any association or institution depends mainly on the effectiveness of the General Secretary. He shall be the Liaison Officer, relating any activities with the Union. The action of the General Secretary shall under the direction and supervision of the President and the Deputy President of the Union. He shall, in addition to the duties vested in him elsewhere in this Constitution, maintain in two separate Minute Books the proceeding of Meetings of the Council and of the Annual and Special General Meetings respectively and shall handle correspondence promptly and tabled at the Council meetings. He shall specially assign Membership Drive to the Chief Organizer, which however, will still remain under his supervision. He shall communicate with each member in activities to perform them for the continuance and progress of the Union.

  5. Assistant Secretary – The Assistant Secretary is the man behind the Progress of the Union. He shall be the Recording Officer of the Union and should assist the General Secretary in all activities to make successful commitment, and specially calling and conducting Council meetings. 

  6. Treasurer – The Treasurer is the Financial Controller and Custodian of the Union funds and the financial discipline of the Union depends on the efficiency of the Treasurer. His official functions shall be under the supervision of the Finance Committee. The Treasurer shall collect, and keep a true and proper accounts of, the finance of the Union and shall maintain the necessary books of accounts for that purpose. He shall submit to the Council a quarter yearly Statement of Accounts and an audited Statement of accounts of the Union for the financial year last ended so that it may be tabled at the last meeting of the Council prior to the AGM.   

  7. Assistant Treasurer – The Assistant Treasurer is the Accounting Officer, a member of the Union’s Finance Committee. He shall keep the members well informed of the financial condition of the Union. And should also provides the necessary discipline for keeping funds in their proper category. 

  8. Chief Organizer – The Chief Organizer is the Convener of the Union and should reactivate the inactive groups. He is responsible of increasing members and also should extend batch groups.   

  9. Sports Secretary – The Sports Secretary shall promote and appoint Coordinators who shall be responsible for the conduct of each Section eg: Cricket, Soccer, Rugger, Basketball, Volleyball, Athletics, Swimming, Tennis, Table Tennis, Billiard, Snooker, etc., which may be expanded from time to time as necessity demands.

  10. Social Secretary – The Social Secretary should promote image building, amity and harmony, recreation and also is responsible of developing the community, environment and welfare of Lasallians. 

  11. Youth Secretary – The Youth Secretary should gather the youth. He should be responsible to motivate the youth by organizing programmes such as Carrier Guidance, vocational Training, etc.

  12. Educational Secretary – The Educational Secretary shall organize programmes to develop the Educational & Cultural affairs of the younger generation of De La Salle College.

  13. Media Secretary – The Media Secretary is the Publicity Officer and he is responsible of publishing the news bulletin quarter yearly.

  14. Auditor – The Auditor who shall be elected at the Annual General Meeting shall audit the Annual Accounts of the Union before the 15th of April. 

 



  1. FUNCTIONS


  1. Loyalty Day – Nineteenth of March the feast of St. Joseph’s day each year shall be mark as the Loyalty day.

  2. Lasallian Day – Fifteenth of May the College day each year shall be mark as the Lasallian Day.

  3. Annual Mass – The Annual Mass shall be held on the feast of St. John Baptist De La Salle day 07th of April.

  4. Annual General Meeting – The Annual General Meeting shall be held on the Nativity day of St. John Baptist De La Salle, 30th of April.

  5. Annual Get-together –The Annual Get-together shall be held on the inaugurated day of the OBU, which is on the 03rd of February.




  1. ADMINISTRATION


  1. Subsidiary Organization – The bodies mentioned below and any other similar bodies recognized, as such by the Council from time to time shall function as subsidiary organizations. Every sub organizations shall frame Rules or Bye-Laws for the proper conduct of its own activities, which shall have the prior approval of the Council meeting. 

  1. Lasallian  Sports  Club

  2. Lasallian  Youth  Movement

  3. Lasallians  In  Action


  1. Batch Groups – The Old Boys of the College can be organize Batch Groups according to age groups or every five years but shall be a member of the Union.

  2. Branches – Overseas branches of the Union may be established with the approval of the Council. There shall be no local branches of the Union. 

  3. Control - The President of the Union shall be the Director / Principle of De La Salle College, who should be one of the Brothers of Christian Schools (FSC) or a Catholic Priest. Provided that a layman becoming the Principle of De La Salle College, in such case the Deputy President of the Union becomes the President of the Union with immediate effect. There-after no such title as Deputy President will continue until the above said is resumed.  

  4. Authority – The Council of the Union shall gather once in every calendar month. In such case if the Council meetings have not been held for a period of three months consecutively, the Chief Organizer of the Union shall have the authority to summon a Special General Meeting giving prior notice as specified in Rule 9.3 foregoing respectively. 

  5. Affiliation – The Union shall be affiliated to the La Sallian Federation of Sri Lanka (Federation of Old Boys of Christian Brothers in Sri Lanka). If at any time there should arise any difference of opinion or divergence which affects our relation with the Federation, action can only be taken by the Union in the form of a substantive Motion presented by the Council at a Special General Meeting and such decision as is passed by a two-thirds majority at this meeting will alone by binding.

  6. Winding Up – Notwithstanding anything to the contrary herein before contained the Union shall not be wound up except by a Special General Meeting specifically convened for that purpose at which the quorum shall be not less than 50 per Centrum (50%) of the members of the Union and unless such winding up is approved by at least two-thirds (2/3) of the members present and voting at such meetings. The Assets of the Union shall, after the payment of all claims, vest in the Director, De La Salle College, Mutwal, to be applied by him at his sole discretion for the furtherance of College Activities.

  7. Matters Not Provided For – In any matter not provided for in the rules, the decision of the Council shall be final and effectual subject to ratification by the general body.  

  8. Rules – No rule shall be altered, amended, added to rescinded or varied in any manner, unless such action is authorized at a General Meeting by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of those present and entitled to vote.

  9. Constitution – Any amended to the Constitution by way of addition, alteration, omission or otherwise shall only be effected by a resolution thereto previously approved by the Council and passed by a two third (2/3) majority of those present and voting, at a Special General Meeting of the Union convened for that purpose upon a decision of the Council. Not less than twenty-one (21) days’ notice of a Special General Meeting shall be given to members by the General Secretary of the Union.

SPIRIT OF THE  CREST



The Motto

 “Indivisa Manent”  meant in English is “Undivided Remain” signifies INSEPEPARABLE BROTHERHOOD, a motto of the La Salle family which the Brothers came to cherish.


The Cross

The cross is the Christian symbol of HOPE and new life. We believe that beyond death there is life;  in despair there is hope.


The Castle

The castle symbolizes NOBILITY. It signifies the high character and qualities splendidly.


The Chevrons

These form part of the coat of arms of the De La Salle family of Rheims, France. There are three bent bars of inverted “V” shapes. They indicate that we should have ZEAL in carrying out our tasks and the courage never to give up.

 

The Star

This five-pointed, radiant star is the worldwide logo of the De La Salle Brothers. It reminds them of the spirit of their institute, the spirit of FAITH. The spirit of faith will lead us to Christ just as the star of Bethlehem led the Wise Men of the East to Christ. The star led the shepherds to Christ on the first Christmas night. The light from stars keeps navigators of ships and aircraft on course. It can be our response to the Gospel directive “Let your light shine before all that they may see your good works and glorify your Father in Heaven”. 



The Laurels

Jesus said “I am come that you may have life and have it to the full”. Laurels are the sign of VICTORY showing that it is possible:

  • to enjoy the process of living by Gospel values

  • to pursue excellence in whatever we do

  • to be of assistance to others

  • to success and distinction win reputation


WAVE

This wavy background behind the star stands for the De La Salle College is situated near the Indian Ocean. It signifies the Lasallians are WEALTHY.

Words by – Rev. Bro. Marcus Felix ,  f.s.c., b.a.

Music by – S. Norbert Rodrigo,  a.v.c.m.


1) La Sallians, all let then arise

To rally round our colours true

And greet in keen and loyal wise

The virile red,  the gentle blue



CHORUS:

        Let’s stand united, children all,

     Come weal, come woe, our way

         And hark to duty’s clarion call

         Throughout the livelong day.


2) With St. La Salle, for friend and guide,

Let’s all drink deep at wisdom’s spring.

To virtue’s path with certain stride,

For Christ’s pure love to learn to cling.


3) La Sallians youth, remember pray,

‘Tis now you must prepare to stand.

With steadfast will in life’s grim fray,

Yea true to God and fatherland.

“INDIVISA MANENT”
