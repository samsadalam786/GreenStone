IBPS RRB PO Prelims Exam Analysis (13th September 2020)
Sept. 17, 2020 1692 Views

View IBPS RRB PO 2020 Exam Analysis Discussion

The IBPS RRB PO Prelims exam on 13th September 2020 was again conducted in 5 shifts. Certain questions were a little lengthy compared to the 12th September exam.

The type of questions did not change and you can see similar questions being asked on 13th as well. Though the paper was a bit more calculative in some shifts.

Let's start with 13th September 2020 Exam Analysis.

The level of the Quants section, was overall moderate, but calculative. DI in all the shifts was mostly calculative & moderate in nature. The other questions were easy - moderate in nature with simple calculations.

Practice a lot in the remaining days before your exam and you will be able to score between 30 - 35 in quants. Work well on your speed & accuracy.

Keeping in mind all the shifts of 12th & 13th September, safe attempts in quants would be somewhere between 28 - 32.

In the Reasoning Section on 13th, the number of questions topic - wise were almost constant with some minor variations here & there.

The Puzzles & Seating Arrangement questions included almost all the varieties - Box Puzzle, Row Arrangement, Floor Based, Book based, etc. These weren't difficult but if not practiced well can feel tricky.

Overall, the Reasoning section was moderate in nature and safe attempts can be between 25 - 30.

Talking of the cut-off, many students have performed well and, so the cut-off can be expected between 58 - 65 (varying from state-to-state).

At last, we would suggest that make the best use of the remaining days before your exam & practice a lot. During your exam, refrain from any sort of guess work & unnecessary switching between questions & sections.
